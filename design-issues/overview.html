Designing or selecting a hypermedia information system requires a balance between functionality, scalability, and ease of use. This document outlines the core criteria and features considered during the development of the WorldWideWeb.

Criteria for Hypermedia Systems

The following features determine the power and flexibility of a web of information:

  • Navigation & Discovery: Techniques including browsing, keyword indexing, and graphical maps.
  • Web Topology: The structural arrangement of nodes and the nature of their links.
  • Multiuser Access: Considerations for protection, collaborative editing, locking, and annotation.
  • Versioning: Mechanisms for tracking previous versions of nodes and the evolution of their relationships.
  • Link Semantics: The ability for links to express specific types of relationships between documents.

The Three Pillars of Interoperability

For different systems to work together within a unified multiverse, agreement is required on three critical issues:

1. Naming and Addressing

A universal system for Naming and Addressing documents is the bedrock of the web.

2. Protocols

The rules of communication. Standard Protocols allow browsers to talk to servers across disparate networks.

3. Document Formats

The Data Formats used to store and transfer content.

Implementation and Optimization

Efficient implementation requires caching, knowbots, and gateways to integrate external systems.
